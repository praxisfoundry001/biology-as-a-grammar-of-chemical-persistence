# Biology as a Grammar of Chemical Persistence: What Entropy Could Not Finish – A Structural Flow Perspective

## What is this?
A bridge / foundation artifact in the biology lane of the Structural Flow library.

This paper asks what biology looks like when it is treated not as a separate starting point, but as a late, narrow regime of chemical persistence under entropy.

## Why should I care?
This artifact gives biology a structural starting point without reducing it to mechanism lists or treating life as exempt from chemistry.

It is useful if you want a clean account of why persistence, cascades, boundary maintenance, copying, and filtering become unavoidable once chemical persistence lasts long enough.

## What is the claim?
The paper argues that biology is not a separate primitive layer.

Instead, where persistence succeeds under entropy, certain structural behaviors become unavoidable:
- recursive persistence
- cascades
- boundary maintenance
- copying under finite capacity
- filtering by failure

## What is this not?
This artifact is not:
- a replacement for chemistry
- a replacement for molecular biology, genetics, or evolutionary theory
- a mechanism-level account of life
- a teleological theory of biological purpose

It is a structural grammar for what biology must look like when chemical persistence succeeds.

## How was this done?
The paper begins from Chance under entropy, then follows the consequences of persistence:
1. persistence becomes recursive
2. recursion propagates as cascades
3. cascades require boundary maintenance
4. finite capacity forces copying
5. copying under entropy produces structural filtering
